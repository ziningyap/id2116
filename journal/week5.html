This week, we looked into coding our p5.js so that the right video will appear
with their corresponding triggers for the individual key video states.


Our key video states had to include the following functions:
+ Videos appearing and playing
+ Videos hidden and paused
+ Videos at normal speed vs sped up

All whilst following the condition that there is smooth transition from each video state to another through the
assigned trigger actions and that only one video would be playing at a time.

Controlling two separate videos concurrently using two different sensors    →   setting two separate conditions so they can run concurrently.

To simplify the data that is sent from the microbit to p5, we decided to have the data read on microbit and converted to a fixed response (i.e. “dim). Less data is required to be sent over through the bluetooth UART service, allowing us to solve the previous 020 error.
